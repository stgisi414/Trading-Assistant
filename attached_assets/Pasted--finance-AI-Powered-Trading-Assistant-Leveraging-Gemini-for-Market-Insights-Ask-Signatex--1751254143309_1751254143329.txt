Analyzing Ethereum: A Beginner's Guide
Since you're adding ETH, let's make sure you're comfortable with your current indicators. For a beginner in crypto trading, sticking with SMA, RSI, and Volume is a smart move. Here's why:

Simple Moving Average (SMA): This helps identify trends. A 7-day SMA will smooth out the short-term price fluctuations of ETH, showing you the overall direction (uptrend or downtrend). Looking at the 7-day SMA alongside a longer-term SMA (like a 20-day or 50-day) can help you spot potential buy or sell signals. For instance, a short-term SMA crossing above a long-term SMA could signal a bullish trend.

Relative Strength Index (RSI): This indicator measures the speed and change of price movements. It ranges from 0 to 100. Generally, an RSI above 70 suggests the asset is overbought (potential sell signal), and below 30 suggests it's oversold (potential buy signal). Remember, RSI can give false signals, so always use it in conjunction with other indicators.

Volume: This tells you how much ETH is being traded. High volume confirms price movements. For example, if the price is going up AND volume is high, it suggests strong buying pressure. Conversely, a price drop with high volume indicates strong selling pressure.

Level Up Your Analysis!
Once you're comfortable with the basics, consider adding more advanced indicators to your Signatex setup. These will give you a more nuanced view of the market:

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): This is a trend-following momentum indicator that identifies changes in the strength, direction, momentum, and duration of a trend in a price series. It's great for spotting potential buy and sell signals.

Bollinger Bands: These show the volatility of the price. When the price touches the upper band, it might be overbought, and when it touches the lower band, it might be oversold.

Stochastic Oscillator: This measures the momentum of price changes to predict potential reversals.

Remember, no indicator is perfect. Always use multiple indicators together and combine technical analysis with fundamental analysis (researching the underlying technology and market sentiment around ETH).
